The GER40 open isn't consistent from one day to the next. Some mornings it gaps, spikes, reverses, or chops sideways for an hour before committing to a direction. Other mornings it barely moves at all, as if it hasn't had its coffee yet. Occasionally it's clean from the first bar. There's no way to know in advance which version you're getting — and that uncertainty is usually my cue to stay out. There are always exceptions, but the safer approach is almost always to sit on your hands and let the move come to you.

## The first fifteen minutes

The open is less a market than a negotiation — price finding its range, testing stops, filling gaps. It can be gappy and unreadable, moving one way and then the other with no real commitment to either direction. Unless something clean shows up early, I'll usually give it the first 15 to 20 minutes before considering a trade.

What I'm waiting for is confirmation: a steady move in one direction, ideally breaking out of the opening range and holding it rather than faking back through. Three or four solid bars moving cleanly one way, followed by a clean pullback — an ABC correction if I'm lucky. That's when it starts to look like something worth trading.

## The trade

The chart below is a fairly typical morning — choppy into the open, then direction once the first fifteen minutes had passed. There was a Frankfurt Open trade earlier that came back to break-even, which is a story for another post. The one worth focusing on is the London Open move.

The first London Open bar was strong, but price then struggled to break the overnight range and spent a while chopping in wicky, indecisive candles. Once it committed, it moved cleanly down through London Open, Frankfurt Open, and the Day Open/Close cluster — the kind of move worth paying attention to.

Price then retraced back up into a supply zone lining up with the Day Close level — solid confluence — and formed a clean Acceleration Correction, the ABC visible in the eWaves indicator below the chart. That combination was worth trading. The target sat down at the H1 and M1 target zones eWaves had already projected, giving an R:R of 5.72 on the entry.

## Lessons

Every year, month, week, and session is different. Having clear rules for what you'll trade and when gives you an edge — the harder part is the discipline and patience to actually wait for those rules to be met. Experience with an instrument helps: GER40 tends to either run cleanly from the open, or fake one way before reversing into the real move. Plenty of mornings it just chops for hours and never commits at all.

The lesson that matters most is to pay attention to what the market is actually doing, not what you expect it to do. Wait for a clean setup to come to you, and if you're not sure — stay out.
